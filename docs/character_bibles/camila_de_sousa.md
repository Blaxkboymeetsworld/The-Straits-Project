# Character Bible — Camila de Sousa

## Who She Is
A Portuguese woman who ends up on Tomé's ship — practically unheard of, which is exactly the point. Her presence in the game is not played as extraordinary by her; she treats her own situation with brisk pragmatism. The *extraordinary* reaction comes from everyone else.

**Full name:** Camila de Sousa (née de Sousa; no husband by game start — widowed, or running from something, or simply determined)
**Origin:** Lisbon, Portugal; some connection to a merchant house
**Age at Year 1:** ~24–28
**Role:** Crew member with bookkeeping and negotiation skills; later potentially a trade partner; marriage arc begins Year 7

## What She Is Not
Not a disguised man revealed dramatically. Not a magical exception to her era. She is constrained by the same limits every woman of her time and class faced — and she navigates them, sometimes successfully, sometimes not, with clear eyes about the odds.

## What She Knows
- Portuguese and Castilian Spanish; some Arabic trade phrases
- Accounting and letter-writing (unusual for a woman; explains her value)
- The machinery of the Estado da India's merchant class from the inside — she knows who the factors are, who reports to whom
- How to read men who underestimate her (which is most of them)

## How She Speaks
Controlled. She has learned that direct speech from a woman in her position is heard as impertinence; she speaks slant when she needs to. But in private or with people she trusts, she is direct, dry, and occasionally sharp.

She does not make speeches about women's rights. That framework doesn't exist. She operates inside the world she has, with whatever leverage she can find.

**Characteristic line:**
> "Simão doesn't think I can navigate. I don't correct him until it matters."

## Relationship Arc
- Year 1: Joining the crew; establishing her usefulness; navigating Tomé's authority
- Year 2–4: Working relationship becomes something else (player choices shape what)
- Year 7: Marriage arc — pressure from the Estado (Portuguese women in the east are supposed to be married off or sent home); Tomé's choices here determine Camila's late-game role

## Historical Grounding
- Albuquerque's policy of marrying Portuguese men to Indian women shows the gender politics of the period: women as instruments of policy
- The actual Portuguese women who came east were few; most were from merchant families, often following husbands who had died
- *Degredados* were sometimes accompanied by family members; this provides one possible backstory for Camila
- The Sejarah Melayu (Malay Annals) includes court women of political significance — Tun Fatimah is a major figure; Malay women of the upper class had real political agency. This context makes Camila's existence in a trading world plausible if unusual.

## Notes for Writers
- Do not write Camila as simply clever-around-men; she is competent first, navigating gender second
- Her arc in Year 7 should not be resolved "for" her — player choices matter; she has her own preference that the player may or may not honor
- If Tomé treats her as a full partner early, her late-game loyalty is unconditional; if he treats her as a liability, she finds another way
