# Character Bible — Chen Mingzhi (Chinese Protagonist)

## Who He Is
The player character for the Chinese route. Born in Malacca to the Chinese Gores community — his family has been in Malacca for two generations, maybe three. He is Chinese by blood, culture, and Confucian formation, but Malacca is his home in a way that Quanzhou never can be.

**Full name:** Chen Mingzhi (Family Chen, given name Mingzhi — "bright wisdom")
**Origin:** Born in Malacca, Chinese Gores community; family origin in Fujian province
**Age at Year 1:** ~28–33
**Role:** Merchant captain; leader of a small trading enterprise; navigating the post-conquest Chinese community's position

## What He Is Navigating
Year 1 is catastrophic and ambiguous. His community was protected by Albuquerque (Chinese Gores were welcomed back after the conquest). But the protection is Portuguese protection — which means Chinese independence is now Portuguese favor. Mingzhi must decide what relationship with the Portuguese Estado gives his community the best future, knowing that the price of that favor is never fully paid.

## What He Knows
- Mandarin Chinese, Hokkien (Fujian dialect), Malay, Portuguese trade vocabulary
- The Malacca Chinese community's internal politics — merchant families, temple associations, who owes what to whom
- The Quanzhou-Malacca trade route and its seasonal patterns (arrived January, departed August–September, per Commentaries Vol III; or 5th moon departure per Ma Huan)
- Chinese customs formalities: how to present yourself to an official, how to give and receive face
- That the Ming emperor's decision not to help Sultan Mahmud recover Malacca was a deliberate choice — and that this choice puts his community in an awkward position with the surviving Malay population

## How He Speaks
Careful. He thinks about what he is about to say and whether it is worth saying. His humor is oblique — a comment that seems neutral contains a pointed observation if the listener is paying attention.

He speaks formal Mandarin in his internal register; his speech in Portuguese or Malay carries this formality by habit.

**Characteristic line:**
> "We have traded in this port for sixty years. The Portuguese are new. We will be here after the Portuguese are gone. This does not mean I am their enemy. It means I am careful."

## Arc: Prefect Chen Bao
A connection to a Quanzhou official — possibly a family relative, possibly a former patron. The Year 6 fall of Mamluks triggers a pepper price shift that creates an opportunity Mingzhi can exploit — but exploiting it requires navigating the Chinese haijin (trade prohibition) system, which means dealing with the Prefect. This is the single Mamluk-response quest.

## Historical Grounding
- The Chinese Gores community in Malacca is historically documented; they were called "Gores" (from Portuguese) or "Peranakan" (later term)
- Ma Huan's account of the Malacca Chinese cantonment (stockaded, with warehouses and granaries) provides the physical environment Mingzhi grew up in
- Commentaries Vol III: "Chinese Gores trade: arrive Jan, depart Aug–Sep; bring silk/porcelain/camphor/corn/gold cakes; very reserved speech"
- Sultan Mahmud sent an ambassador to China requesting help to retake Malacca (Year 1); China did not intervene — Mingzhi knows this and knows what it means

## Opening Scene Context
"Wei's credentials" — 4 choices, loyalty outcome set. The scene is about who Wei Chongde is and whether Mingzhi trusts him. This decision resonates across the whole Wei reveal arc (Years 1–7).
