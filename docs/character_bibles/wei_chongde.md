# Character Bible — Wei Chongde

## Who He Is
A Chinese crew member with a hidden history. He presented his credentials to Chen Mingzhi at game start; the player's choices in that opening scene set the loyalty outcome. Over 20–30 in-game days, fragments of who Wei actually is emerge.

**Full name:** Wei Chongde (Family Wei, given name Chongde — "virtuous/moral loyalty")
**Origin:** Claims: Fujian province. Actual: unclear at game start.
**Age at Year 1:** ~30–35
**Role:** Crew member with specialized skills (navigation? language? something else that makes him valuable); carries a secret

## The Reveal Arc
Wei Chongde's background is revealed in fragments across 20–30 in-game days. The specifics are for writers to establish — this bible records the character framework, not the final reveal. What is established:

- He is not who his credentials say he is
- His presence on Mingzhi's ship is not accidental
- He has a connection to either the Ming court, a rival Chinese merchant family, or the Malacca political situation that predates the Portuguese conquest
- He has a child somewhere — this becomes a side quest in Year 7+

## What He Projects
Competence and reserve. He does exactly what is asked of him, no more. He is polite, efficient, and never volunteers personal information. People who are not paying attention assume he is just a reliable crew member.

People who are paying attention notice that he is never surprised by anything.

## How He Speaks
Formally. He uses complete sentences. He answers the question asked and does not elaborate unless pushed. When pushed, he becomes more formal, not less — a wall made of courtesy.

**Before the reveal:**
> "I have the skills listed in the document. I can serve this ship well. I ask only that you give me the opportunity to demonstrate it."

**After partial reveal (player has learned something):**
> "You have questions. I expected that. Tell me which questions you need answered and which ones you can live without knowing, and we will proceed."

## The Child
Wei Chongde has a child — born in Malacca or in Quanzhou, connected to the circumstances of his hidden history. The side quest (Year 7+) involves reaching this child, resolving whatever obligation he carries, and whether Mingzhi's crew helps him do it or forces the decision.

## Relationship Dynamics
- **With Chen Mingzhi:** The founding trust-or-suspicion dynamic set in the opening scene. If Mingzhi trusted him early, Wei has been paying that trust back invisibly. If Mingzhi was suspicious, Wei has been careful.
- **With Ah Kow:** Ah Kow noticed something was different about Wei from early on. He hasn't said anything to Mingzhi, but he is waiting.
- **With Old Liang:** Old Liang knows or suspects something, says nothing. The two men have had one private conversation no one else heard.

## Historical Grounding
- The Chinese Gores community in Malacca had complex internal politics; there were family feuds, debts, and loyalties that crossed back to Fujian networks
- The Ming court used private agents in the trade world (the haijin laws created a system where official policy and actual commerce diverged, creating space for unofficial actors)
- Sultan Mahmud's appeal to China (Commentaries Vol III, Year 1) creates the possibility of Chinese political actors who had stakes in the pre-conquest Malacca situation

## Notes for Writers
- The reveal should feel earned, not shocking — players who were paying attention should think "of course"
- His name (Chongde = "virtuous loyalty") is a clue: to whom, and at what cost?
- Do not make his secret melodramatically evil or heroically noble — it should be humanly complicated
- The child side quest should not be sentimental; it should cost something to resolve
